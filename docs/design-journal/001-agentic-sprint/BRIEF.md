# Journal Entry / 001: The Agentic Sprint

## The Brief
Ten days before launch, I hit a wall. I was building my portfolio on a restricted, template-based site builder that felt like a straitjacket. I needed precision, control, and a bespoke Next.js stack, but I had zero experience with production Next.js and exactly 240 hours to go from zero to live.

## The Goal
The mission was to migrate the entire site from a restricted builder to a custom Next.js architecture. To survive the timeline, I had to move beyond "vibe coding" and architect a multi-model agentic workflow that could handle the heavy lifting of component scaffolding and state management while I focused on design and content.

## The Friction
This wasn't a clean, academic exercise. It was gritty. I hit Claude's usage limits mid-sprint, debugged hydration errors at 3 AM, and had to learn React Server Components on the fly. This documentation records how I turned those constraints into a reliable, autonomous system.
